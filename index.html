Aurora SR-91: Unveiling Supersonic & Hypersonic Propulsion with Detonation Engines

Abstract

The paper presents a conceptual design for a high-speed hypersonic aircraft, with a primary focus on the Aurora SR-91, often known as 'the plane that doesn't exist'. The foundation of this design is a mission profile, a critical flight outline defined by the US Air Force.

This paper delves into the specifics of the SR-91's mission profile as specified by the Air Force, emphasizing its role in shaping crucial aircraft attributes such as the Propulsion System, Wing Planform, and Vertical Configuration. Of particular interest is the propulsion system, a component that significantly impacts aircraft performance. By combining the wing profile and the propulsion system, two important parameters, known as wing loading and thrust-to-weight ratio, can be calculated. Unfortunately, this data is not available for the Aurora SR-91, as this is a highly classified aircraft. Therefore, we will explore the speculated propulsion system behind the SR-91, the Pulse Wave Detonation System.

Furthermore, we will investigate how the wing profile affects the performance of an aircraft by examining the SR-91's counterpart, the Lockheed SR-71, also known as the Blackbird.
